What is cLI?

Championship Leverage Index measures how important a regular-season game is to a team's World Series chances. A cLI of 1.00 means the game matters about as much as an Opening Day game. Higher values mean the game swings the team's championship odds more. A September game between two contenders might be 3.0+. Game 7 of the World Series would be around 170.

How it works

For each team in each game, the calculator runs Monte Carlo simulations of the rest of the season — once assuming the team wins, once assuming they lose. Every remaining game is a coin flip. The full postseason bracket is simulated. The difference in World Series win probability between the two scenarios, divided by the Opening Day baseline (0.006), gives the cLI.

Credits

cLI was developed by Dave Studeman and Sky Andrecheck. Dan Hirsch (The Baseball Gauge / Baseball-Reference) built the implementation that popularized nightly cLI numbers. This calculator replicates that methodology in the browser using the free MLB Stats API.
